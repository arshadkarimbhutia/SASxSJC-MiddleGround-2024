Middle-Ground Event

Hosted by the Sasonians' Literary Club & St. Joseph Convent's Literary Club

Event Overview

The Middle Ground is a formal intellectual discussion or a synthesis of a debate, aiming for a more casual approach to find common ground where both sides can agree. The Middle-Ground event took place on May 22, 2024, and was a collaborative effort between the Sasonians' Literary Club and St. Joseph's Convent's literary club. Participants from both clubs engaged in this enriching discussion, focused on five diverse prompts on GenZ and presented distinct perspectives.

More About Middle-Ground

The Contrasting Teams

There are two teams, each consisting of about 5 people, who either agree or disagree on a given prompt based on a particular topic. They seek to find a middle ground through the expression of their contradicting ideals or stances on the topic, within a set time period by the moderator.

Polarized Perspectives

The discussion begins with a participant from team A presenting their views, followed by a participant from team B presenting their counter or introducing new perspectives. This continues until everyone has spoken at least once, setting the stage for the discussion.

Requirements & Rules

  • Participants must raise their hand if they wish to speak.
  • The moderators can call on a student to present their thoughts.
  • Students in the audience must raise their hand if they wish to ask something to the participants on stage.
  • The moderator chooses who will speak from the students raising their hands.
  • The main motive of the participants must be to reach a middle ground.
  • If the moderator feels a participant is speaking out of context, he/she can warn them.
  • Students are allowed to carry notes but must not read directly from them.
  • Participants must address opinions raised by the crowd if directed towards them.
